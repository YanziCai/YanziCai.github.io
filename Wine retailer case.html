Wine Retailer Case Assignment

Yanzi Cai, Ao Zhang, Sizhu Wu, Dong Li, Keyan Wang

Project Background

  • Wine retailer experiment
    • Test setting: email to retailer customers
    • Unit : customer (email address)
    • Treatments: email, holdout
    • Reponse: open, click and 1-month purchase ($)
    • Selection: all active customers
    • Assignment: randomly assigned (1/2 each) to email or control (ctrl)
  • Dataset: “data.csv” (total 12 varibles)
    • user_id: user ID
    • group: email ot ctrl
    • respond: includes “open”,“click” and “purch” three columns.
    • kind of wine: includes “chard”, “sav”, “syrah” and “cab” four columns.
    • past_purch: amount of past purchase in dollars.
    • last_purch: days since last purchase.
    • visits: how many times visit

Analysis task

A.Evalute average causal effect.
B.Slice and dice analysis to illustrate the potential for targeting on responses for this email campaign.
C.“Individual-level” conditional causal effect estimates to be used along with the margin and cost information to score each customer.

Part B. Slice and Dice Analysis
  • For each variable, identify the best cutoff point, where each variable has a significant impact on purchase decision seperately.
cutoff = as.data.frame(matrix(0,7,3))
colnames(cutoff) = c("variable","p-value","number")

#past purchase
num = seq(0,170,5)
dataTry = data
num2 = 1:35
for (i in 1:35){
  dataTry$pastPurch = (data$past_purch > num[i])
  lm = summary(lm(purch~group*pastPurch,data=dataTry))
  num2[i] = lm$coefficients[4,4]
  cutoff[1,1] = "past purchase"
  cutoff[1,2] = num2[which.min(num2)]
  cutoff[1,3] = num[which.min(num2)] #160
  finalPos = which.min(num2)
}
